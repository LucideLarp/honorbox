Plain-language terms for buying from this store. Last updated 2026-07-20.

## The seller

Products here are sold by the HonorBox maintainer, who is the merchant of
record for this store. Payments are processed by Stripe, and this site never
sees or stores your card details. Contact: **honorbox@proton.me**.

The project publishes no company name or legal identity behind that address.
[Support](./support.html) sets out plainly what that does and does not mean
for a buyer.

## What you're buying

Each product, [HonorBox Pro](./honorbox-pro.html) ($29) and
[Crew](./crew.html) ($19), is access to a private GitHub repository containing
code, themes, agents, and written guides.

Checkout asks for one thing beyond payment: your GitHub username. Delivery is
an invitation to that product's repository, sent to the account you named,
usually within minutes and always within a few hours. You need a GitHub
account to receive it. Access is permanent and includes future updates to the
repository.

The free HonorBox core is separate and MIT-licensed.

## Refunds

30 days from purchase, no questions asked. Email honorbox@proton.me, reply to
your Stripe receipt, or open an issue on the public repository, and the payment
will be refunded in full to the original payment method. **Repository access
and the license end when a refund is issued.** Refunds are honored in good
faith; orders that abuse the policy (for example, repeat purchase-and-refund
patterns) may be declined and disputed through Stripe.

The [refund policy](./refunds.html) has the detail, including what happens
after 30 days.

## Delivery problems

If your invite doesn't arrive within a few hours: check that the GitHub username
you entered at checkout is correct, then email honorbox@proton.me or open an
issue. Wrong-username orders are fixed manually. If delivery can't be completed,
you get a full refund. The recovery path is written out step by step on the
[support page](./support.html).

## If the project stops

The free engine is MIT-licensed, and that license is irrevocable. Paid
delivery is a private repository your GitHub account holds, and the code in it
does not check a license, contact any server, or expire, so it keeps working
whether or not this project does. What would end is future updates. Nothing
here is a promise that the project will continue, and nothing about your
access depends on that promise.

## Liability

The software and guides are provided as-is, without warranty. To the extent
permitted by law, liability is limited to the amount you paid.

## Tax

Prices are in USD, and the price shown is the total you pay. No sales tax or
VAT is calculated, added, or collected at checkout, and no tax ID is
requested. Your Stripe receipt is the record of the transaction. If your own
jurisdiction requires you to account for tax on an imported digital purchase,
that obligation is yours.

This paragraph describes what this store does when selling to you. It is
separate from what HonorBox the product does for sellers who run it: those
sellers are their own merchants of record, which the [tax guide](./tax.html)
