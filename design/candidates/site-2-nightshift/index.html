How the honor box works

  1. Fork the stand

    Fork the free, MIT-licensed engine and edit one config file. Your storefront builds to GitHub Pages; hosting costs $0, forever.

  2. Put out your goods

    Create a Stripe Payment Link for each product and drop the URL in the config. Buyers pay you directly on your own Stripe account. Nobody stands between you and your balance.

  3. Let the box collect

    A scheduled GitHub Action polls Stripe, invites each buyer's GitHub account to your private product repo, and keeps your books. No webhooks, no server, no database.

Who sells this way: indie hackers and SaaS builders, template and boilerplate makers, coding-course creators, CLI and API authors, open-source maintainers funding their work, agencies productizing internal tools, students selling a first project. The one hard requirement: your buyers need GitHub accounts, because delivery is a repo invite. Selling physical goods, ebooks for lay readers, or to a general consumer audience? A merchant-of-record platform will serve you better; the first guide below compares them.

Straight answers

What exactly do I get when I buy?

Your GitHub account is invited to the product's private repository, usually within minutes and always within a few hours. You keep access permanently, updates included. If the invite doesn't arrive, email honorbox@proton.me or open an issue and it will be fixed or refunded.

Why is there no instant download?

Because the product is private-repo access, which a download link can't be: per-buyer grants, clean revocation on refund, updates included forever. A scheduled job sends the invite, usually within minutes, with zero infrastructure to run or break. Sellers who want seconds can turn on the optional webhook mode. A download URL is instant, and then uncontrollable.

Do my buyers need GitHub accounts?

Yes. Delivery is a repo invite, so every buyer needs one. Fine for code, templates, courses aimed at technical people, and tools. The wrong channel for lay-reader ebooks, physical goods, or general consumers. Better to know that before your first failed delivery.

Do I need HonorBox Pro to use the engine?

No. The free core is a complete store: one theme, checkout, fulfillment, docs. Pro adds the offline license-key module, the premium themes, and multi-product catalog patterns. Buying it funds the free core.

Does the fulfillment bot need my full Stripe secret key?

No. A restricted key that can only read Checkout Sessions runs the whole poll; it cannot move money, refund, or create anything. Pair it with a fine-grained GitHub token valid for just the product repo. The least-privilege guide in the docs shows the exact toggles.

What about VAT and sales tax?

HonorBox is not a merchant of record. You sell on your own Stripe account, so tax obligations are yours. That's workable for most small sellers under registration thresholds, and Stripe Tax can automate it beyond that. The tax doc walks through what a small seller actually owes.

Refunds?

30 days, no questions asked, refunded through Stripe to your original payment method. Repository access ends when a refund is issued.

Who's behind this?

HonorBox is an independent project. Support: honorbox@proton.me or GitHub issues; both are answered fast.
