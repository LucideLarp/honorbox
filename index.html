How the honor box works

  1. Fork the stand

    Fork the free, MIT-licensed engine. Your storefront builds to GitHub Pages — hosting costs nothing, forever.

  2. Put out your goods

    Create a Stripe Payment Link for your product and drop the URL in one config file. Buyers pay you directly; nobody stands between you and your Stripe balance.

  3. Let the box collect

    A scheduled GitHub Action polls Stripe, invites each buyer's GitHub account to your private product repo, and keeps your books. No webhooks, no server, no database.

Straight answers

What exactly do I get when I buy Pro?

Your GitHub account is invited to the private honorbox-pro repository — usually within 30 minutes, always within a few hours. You keep access permanently, updates included. If the invite doesn't arrive, email honorbox@proton.me or open an issue and it will be fixed or refunded.

Why is there no instant download?

Delivery is a private-repo invite because it's the only channel that stays access-controlled. A cron job runs the fulfillment — that's the honest cost of a $0/month stack, and it's usually minutes.

Do I need HonorBox Pro to use the engine?

No. The free core is a complete store: one theme, checkout, fulfillment, docs. Pro adds the premium themes, multi-product catalog patterns, and the offline license-key module — and buying it funds the free core.

What about VAT and sales tax?

HonorBox is not a merchant of record. You sell on your own Stripe account, so tax obligations are yours — fine for most small sellers under registration thresholds, and Stripe Tax can automate it beyond that. The docs cover this honestly instead of pretending it away.

Refunds?

30 days, no questions asked, refunded through Stripe to your original payment method. Repository access ends when a refund is issued.

Who's behind this?

HonorBox is an independent project. Support: honorbox@proton.me or GitHub issues — both are answered fast.

This store dogfoods its own engine — the checkout you go through is the same one your buyers would. If something feels off, that's a bug report we want.
